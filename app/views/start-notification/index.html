Find a solution to your employment dispute

Use this form to begin the legal process of notifying Acas that you want:

  • a certificate to make a claim to an employment tribunal
  • early conciliation - where Acas speaks with you and your employer separately to try and resolve the problem without going to an employment tribunal

Check you’ve followed the right steps before starting.

Step 1: Check your time limit

You usually have 3 months minus 1 day to notify Acas. This will depend on the type of work problem you have. Find out when your time limit starts.

Step 2: Check you’ve followed the right procedure with your employer

Problems at work can often be resolved by speaking with your employer.

Before starting the form you should have either:

  • raised a grievance - this is where you make a formal complaint to your employer
  • raised an appeal - if you think you were dismissed unfairly

What early conciliation is

Early conciliation is a free service where Acas speaks with you and your employer separately to:

help you reach an agreement avoid you having to go to an employment tribunal

The benefits

Early conciliation is much quicker and less stressful than going to an employment tribunal because it:

  • lasts a maximum of 6 weeks - you can wait up to 1 year for a tribunal hearing
  • can help keep a good relationship with your employer
  • gives you a wider choice of outcomes than a tribunal, and they’re still legally binding

Step 4: Start the form to notify Acas about your work problem

Use this form to:

  • get a certificate to make a claim to an employment tribunal
  • try early conciliation before making a claim

You must provide:

  • your full name - for example, the name on your passport or driving licence
  • the name and address of the organisation or employer - find this on a recent letter, payslip, contract, P60 or P45
