Get early conciliation or a certificate for an employment tribunal

Use this form to get:

  • early conciliation – where Acas speaks with you and your employer separately to try and resolve the problem without going to an employment tribunal
  • a certificate to make a caim to an employment tribunal

Check you’ve followed the right steps before starting.

Step 1: Check your time limit

There are time limits for employment tribunal claims. You need to complete this form within the time limit – this is usually 3 months minus 1 day. Find out when your time limit starts.

Step 2: Check you’ve followed the right procedure with your employer

Problems at work can often be resolved by speaking with your employer.

Before starting the form, where possible try to either:

  • raise a grievance – this is where you make a formal complaint to your employer
  • raise an appeal – if you think you were dismissed unfairly

If you did not try to resolve the problem with your employer

You could lose up to 25% of any compensation you might receive if you make a claim to an employment tribunal.

Make sure you have enough time to try this step. Your tribunal time limit will not get extended for any time you spend trying to resolve the problem with your employer.

Step 3: Decide if you want early conciliation

Early conciliation is a free service where Acas speaks with you and your employer separately to:

  • help you reach an agreement
  • avoid you having to go to an employment tribunal

The benefits

Most claims are resolved through Acas conciliation.

Early conciliation is much quicker and less stressful than going to an employment tribunal because:

  • it lasts a maximum of 6 weeks – you can wait up to 1 year for a tribunal hearing
  • it can help keep a good relationship with your employer
  • you can agree outcomes that you cannot get from a tribunal, for example a job reference

Step 4: Get support and advice

Experiencing a work problem can be stressful. If you need support or advice, you can:

  • contact the Acas helpline – to talk through what the law says and how it relates to you
  • get emotional support
  • speak to a trade union representative, if you're a member of a trade union
  • get legal advice – for example using a solicitor

Step 5: Start the form

Use this form to:

  • try early conciliation before making a claim
  • get a certificate to make a claim to an employment tribunal

You will need the name and address of the organisation or employer – find this on a recent letter, payslip, contract, P60 or P45.

The form will automatically save so you can return to it if you need to.
